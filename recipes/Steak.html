Pan-Seared Steak

Ingredients

  • 1-2 steaks (such as ribeye, New York Strip, or filet mignon), about 1 inch thick
  • Salt and black pepper, to taste
  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil or claified butter (ghee)
  • Garlic cloves (smashed)
  • Fresh rosemary or thyme sprigs
  • Butter (for basting)

Steps

  1. Take the steaks out of the refrigerator and let them come up to room temperature for about 30 minutes. This allows for even cooking.
  2. Pat the steaks dry with a paper towel. Season both sides generously with salt and pepper.
  3. Heat a skillet (preferably cast iron) over high heat. Add the vegetable oil or clarified butter and let it get hot but not smoking.
  4. Carefully place the steaks in the hot skillet, laying them away from you to avoid oil splatter. Cook the steaks without moving them for about 3-4 minutes or until a golden crust forms on the bottom.
  5. Flip the steaks using tongs and cook for another 3-4 minutes for medium-rare doneness. Adjust the cooking time based on your desired level of doneness.
  6. Add smashed garlic cloves and fresh rosemary or thyme sprigs to the skillet. Add a pat of butter to the skillet and let it melt. Tilt the skillet slightly and use a spoon to continuously baste the steaks with melted butter and aromatics for about 1-2 minutes.
  7. Remove the steaks from the skillet and let them rest on the cutting board or a plate for about 5 minutes. This allows for the juices to redistribute and result in a juicier steak.
  8. Slice the steak against the grain (perpendicular to the muscle fibers) into thin slices.
  9. Serve the steak slices immediately, optionally with a side of your choice, such as roasted vegetables, mashed potatoes, or fresh salad.
